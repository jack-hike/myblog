  list:
    - title: "Is Tap to Pay secure?"
      content: |
        Tap to Pay is a secure contactless payment method. Tap to Pay payments are taken when your customer taps their card or digital wallet on your phone’s NFC (Near field communication) reader, when prompted by an application to process a payment. During this process, a unique, single-use-use code, known as a token, is created for each transaction, so that your customer’s card information is not stored or copied locally. 

        With NadaPay Tap to Pay, every payment adheres to the strictest industry standards of security, including Level 1 PCI DSS compliance.

    - title: "What devices are compatible with Tap to Pay?"
      content: "NadaPay Tap to Pay is compatible with any NFC-enabled Android smartphone, or iPhone model XS or later operating with iOS 16.4. To accept payments using Tap to Pay, your phone will also require the NadaPay App, and an approved NadaPay Account."

    - title: "How do I set up Tap to Pay on my phone?"
      content: |

        To set up Tap to Pay on your phone, download the free NadaPay App and log in to your NadaPay Account. Then, select Tap to Pay from the navigation menu on the NadaPay App.

         - Tap <b>Set Up Now</b>
         - Assign a unique name for your device and tap <b>Next</b>
         - Choose a site for your device and tap <b>Next</b>. Or, you can create a new site if required.
         - Confirm if GST should be applied to transactions, and then tap <b>Next</b>
         - Confirm if you want to enable surcharges to apply to your Tap to Pay transactions, then tap <b>Next</b>
         - You can choose to enable tipping, then tap <b>Next</b>
         - Review and configure your digital receipts, then tap <b>Finish Setup</b>

         For iPhone specific instructions see here, or for Android specific instructions, see here.



    - title: "What types of payments can I accept/make with Tap to Pay?"
      content: "You can accept a wide range of contactless payments with Tap to Pay. This includes taking payments from physical contactless credit and debit cards issued by Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, and UnionPay. You can also accept contactless payments from digital wallets such as Apple Pay and Google Pay using your smartphone or NFC-enabled device (such as an Apple Watch)."
    - title: "What should I do if my payment fails?"
      content: |
       If your Tap to Pay payment fails, ensure your customer is placing their contactless card directly near your phone’s NFC reader. Try both physical and digital wallet payments. If you need further assistance, please, contact NadaPay Support."
